Research

Model reduction & hyperreduction for convection-dominated PDEs

I develop optimization-based reduced-order models that track moving features such as shocks and sharp fronts by smoothly deforming the domain. These approaches enable accurate, low-dimensional models for convection-dominated flows at a fraction of the computational cost of full simulations.

AI and surrogate models for flood and hazard prediction

I design physics-aware deep learning surrogates that map rainfall and gauge observations to flood inundation maps using only a small number of expensive hydrodynamic simulations. Current projects include learning surrogates for rainfall-driven inundation and for extreme flood events with real gauge data.

Neural dynamics & biologically constrained learning

In collaboration with colleagues in neural dynamics and computing, I study how biologically plausible plasticity rules can support learning in recurrent neural networks under realistic constraints.
