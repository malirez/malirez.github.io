About

I am an applied and computational mathematician working on reduced-order models for convection-dominated PDEs and machine-learning surrogates for environmental hazards such as flooding. I completed my Ph.D. in Aerospace & Mechanical Engineering at the University of Notre Dame and have held postdoctoral positions at the University of Notre Dame and at MIT (EAPS).

Publications

Model reduction of convection-dominated PDEs via optimization-based implicit feature tracking
Journal of Computational Physics, 473 (2023)

A reduced-order modelling method that tracks moving features, such as shocks, by smoothly deforming the domain. This allows a small reduced model to capture sharp fronts accurately while greatly reducing simulation cost.

Read paper

Accelerated solutions of convection-dominated PDEs using implicit feature tracking and empirical quadrature
International Journal for Numerical Methods in Fluids, 96(1), 102–124 (2024)

Combines implicit feature tracking with an empirical quadrature hyperreduction method to obtain reduced-order models that are both accurate and very fast for convection-dominated flows.

Read paper · arXiv

Learning surrogate rainfall-driven inundation models with few data
arXiv:2411.19323 (2024)

A deep learning surrogate (based on ResNet-style networks) that maps rainfall inputs to flood inundation maps using only a small number of expensive hydrodynamic simulations.

arXiv

Oja’s plasticity rule overcomes several challenges of training neural networks under biological constraints
with Navid Shervani-Tabar & Robert Rosenbaum, arXiv:2408.08408

Shows that adding Oja’s synaptic plasticity rule to error-driven learning yields neural networks that are biologically plausible, stable to train, and can perform well even with limited data.

arXiv

AI-driven surrogate for extreme flood inundation maps with real gauge value prediction
In preparation, planned arXiv submission (2025)

Ongoing work on an AI surrogate that predicts extreme flood inundation maps and water depths from real gauge data and rainfall, aiming to reproduce rare but high-impact events efficiently.
