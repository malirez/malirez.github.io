Research

PDE-constrained optimization · Model reduction · Physics-informed AI · Environmental hazards

Model Reduction & Hyperreduction for Convection-Dominated PDEs

Standard reduced-order models (ROMs) struggle with convection-dominated problems because solutions contain sharp fronts and shocks that cannot be captured efficiently by linear subspaces. I develop optimization-based implicit feature tracking methods that smoothly deform the computational domain so that the moving feature stays aligned with the mesh, enabling compact, accurate low-dimensional representations.

Combined with empirical quadrature / hyperreduction, these approaches achieve dramatic speedups — full accuracy at a fraction of the cost of high-fidelity simulations — making parameter studies and real-time queries feasible for complex fluid problems.

PDE-constrained optimization Implicit feature tracking Empirical quadrature Galerkin ROM Conservation laws

Physics-Aware Surrogate Models for Flood & Hazard Prediction

High-fidelity hydrodynamic simulations of flood inundation are extremely costly — prohibitively so for real-time forecasting or uncertainty quantification. I design deep learning surrogates that learn the map from rainfall and gauge observations to inundation maps using only a small number of expensive simulator runs.

Current work focuses on physics-constrained architectures that respect the underlying hydraulics, data-efficient training strategies for scarce simulation budgets, and generalisation to extreme or out-of-distribution events with real gauge data.

Deep learning Few-data learning Rainfall-runoff Flood inundation Physics-informed ML

Neural Dynamics & Biologically Constrained Learning

We studied how biologically plausible optimization methods, particularly Oja's rule, can support learning in fully connected and recurrent neural networks under realistic biological constraints (local computations, absence of mini-batching, and precise initialization).

Our findings show that Oja's rule stabilizes neural activations, maintains richer activation subspaces, prevents signal explosion or vanishing, and enhances short-term memory in recurrent networks. These results pave the way for more brain-like artificial learning.

Plasticity rules Recurrent networks Oja's rule Biologically plausible ML Hebbian learning
